About Integral Coaching

"We call our work integral because we include everything about the client and the client’s world in our coaching. This includes what the client is aware of along with her potential—what could be brought about through focused, skillful methods. Our coaches bring forth a view of the client that includes how the client represents herself and also investigates her social world, habits, relationship with her body, the quality of self-care, the amount of attention and energy available to take on change, and much more."

The aim of Integral Coaching is to use your entire world as material for developing self-generating, self-sustaining practices that will be relevant through the arc of your career and life. Integral Coaching is not coaching for compliance, or coaching for accountability. It is deeply humanistic work that opens up insights and possibilities for a path forward.

I've experienced the power of Integral Coaching personally. As part of my training I started working with an Integral Coach in September of 2018 and the work has been transformational. What is the Difference Between Coaching and Therapy?

The International Coaching Federation (ICF) defines coaching in the following way: "Partnering with clients in a thought provoking and creative process that inspires them to maximize their personal and professional potential." Coaching focuses on what is happening in the present to help clients build capacity to grow in all areas of their life.

Therapy generally looks back at past experiences and traumas to understand current behaviors. Depending on the type of professional therapy one seeks, different approaches and methodologies are used in healing and letting go of the past in order to cultivate a more stable and positive future.

If you want to go deeper on this topic try the paper Coaching Versus Therapy: A Perspective from the American Psychological Association.
