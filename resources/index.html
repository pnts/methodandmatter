Recommended Reading

A list of books for leaders and seekers. Please note that the links below are affiliate links. Any proceeds generated will be donated to The Association of Ramaytush Ohlone.

  • The Heart Aroused
  • David Whyte
  • What happens when you invite a poet's keen sense of observation into the corporate world? A poetic and unflinching look at the ways in which the business world forces our soul underground, and how we can reclaim it. Particularly poignant for Beowful aficionados.
  • link

  • Radically Condensed Instructions for Being Just as You Are
  • J Matthews
  • An incredibly straightforward and short intro to non-duality. The basic premise is that all we are is our experience in the present moment. The writing is conversational, clear, and insightful with moments of bright humor. In times of stress, I find this book incredibly comforting.
  • link

  • The Fifth Discipline
  • Peter M. Senge
  • The biggest threat facing companies today isn't innovation or competition, it's the inability of organizations to learn. Using a systems approach, Senge identies 5 core disciplines that learning organizations must embrace. Every year or so I re-read Chapter 8: Personal Mastery for the reminder that we must approach our lives as a creative work.
  • link

  • Leadership Embodiment
  • Wendy Palmer & Janet Crawford
  • How we sit and stand can change the way we speak and think. What is it to be embodied, and how can leaders use embodiment to be more effective? Palmer and Crawford have the answers. Includes illustrated embodiment practices as well asn an look at the neuroscience of embodiment.
  • link

  • Difficult Conversations
  • Douglas Stone, Bruce Patton, Sheila Heen
  • Not to be confused with the book 'Crucial Conversations', this is one of the best books I've read when it comes to how to have a hard conversation.
  • link

  • The Art of Possibility
  • Rosamund & Benjamin Zander
  • link

  • Let Your Life Speak
  • Parker J Palmer
